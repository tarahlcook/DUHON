The McCormick Ranch Connection & Amenities

You asked directly: does this property qualify for any McCormick Ranch amenities? Here's the honest answer — and it surprises a lot of buyers.

McCormick Ranch is not an amenity club.

The MRPOA is a master association that maintains shared infrastructure — the lakes, greenbelts, street medians, 2,500+ mature trees, and community-wide architectural standards. Your $268.14/year buys into the upkeep and character of the Ranch, not a member clubhouse, gym, or pool. There is no MRPOA recreation center that residents check into.

What McCormick Ranch does give you is one of Scottsdale's best lifestyle settings, mostly through public amenities that run through and around the community: Publicly Verified

  • The Indian Bend Wash Greenbelt — an ~11-mile multiuse path system (City of Scottsdale, public) with lakes, parks, and 24+ crossings, threading right through McCormick Ranch.
  • McCormick-Stillman Railroad Park — a short walk from Artesia.
  • Two 18-hole golf courses at McCormick Ranch Golf Club — these are public / pay-to-play, operated separately from the POA; membership/greens fees are not an HOA benefit.
  • Lakes with boating allowed on specific lakes under MRPOA permit rules (size/permit limits apply; confirm whether any lake access attaches to Artesia).
  • Community events and a well-kept, tree-canopied streetscape maintained by the POA.
✓
Your resort amenities come from Artesia, not McCormick Ranch
The heated pool, heated spa/hot tub, BBQ ramadas, 10-acre private park, gated/guarded entry, elevators, and underground parking are all Artesia sub-association amenities. That's what your $594.34/month funds.

Bottom line: the property "qualifies" for the maintained common environment and public greenbelt/lake system of McCormick Ranch, but there is no separate members-only McCormick Ranch facility to access. The country-club-style amenities a buyer experiences here belong to Artesia.

★ Headline — the governing document changed on June 12, 2026

The current declaration is the Second Amended & Restated Declaration of Condominium for Artesia, recorded June 12, 2026 by Artesia Multifamily Owner LLC (a Delaware entity) with the HOA joining. It introduces large "Planning Area" parcels (PA-1, PA-2, PA-3) designated for separate ownership and operated by the declarant as a multifamily / rental program, alongside the individually-owned residence units and townhomes.

Why it matters to you: recording this declaration restarted a Period of Declarant Control — the declarant controls the HOA board until the earlier of (a) 90 days after 75% of all possible units are conveyed, or (b) four years after it stops offering units (and a Planning Area isn't deemed "offered out" until building starts there or five years pass). In practice, owners may have limited board-voting power for years, and one institutional entity holds large blocks of units — the exact pattern that can break VA / FHA / conventional project approval. Confirm current approval and warrantability with Phil before removing contingencies.

Restrictions & rules that affect daily living

  • Leasing & short-term rentals
    Only a whole dwelling may be leased, and no rental shorter than three full calendar months — short-term / Airbnb-style use is barred. Leases must be in writing, bound to the CC&Rs, and you must notify the HOA of dates and occupants. Leasing is also subject to the City of Scottsdale short-term-rental ordinance. (The declarant's own rental program on the Planning Areas is exempt from these owner limits.)
  • Pets
    A reasonable number of common domestic house pets; maximum two dogs per dwelling regardless of size or weight; six-foot leash outside the unit; no nuisance, odor, or excessive noise. (The 2009 version capped dogs at 40 lbs; the 2026 version replaced that with the two-dog limit.)
  • Renovations & alterations
    Interior, non-structural changes are generally allowed without board sign-off (you're liable for any damage to other units/common elements) — but flooring and ceiling changes require board approval, and any structural change needs board approval plus an Arizona-licensed architect/engineer certifying structural integrity. Budget approval time into any reno plan.
  • Parking & storage
    Garage / underground parking and storage lockers are assigned to the unit and cannot be leased separately. A private garage must always keep room for two vehicles (no storage that blocks that); no parking on garage driveways.
  • Signs & lockboxes
    No exterior-visible "For Sale / For Rent" or other signs without board approval, and no real-estate lockboxes left on unit doors or windows — worth knowing for showing logistics now and at resale.
  • Window coverings & exterior look
    No foil, mirrored, or reflective window materials without approval; replacement coverings must match the approved color, texture, and size so the building's exterior stays uniform.
  • Nuisance & golf course
    No nuisances or exterior sound devices (except security). The adjacent McCormick Ranch golf course is not part of the condo and grants no membership or use rights; owners near it accept the risk of stray balls. Your resort amenities (pool, spa, ramadas, park) come from Artesia itself.

Money & governance procedures

  • What the $7,132 fee actually is
    At every sale the buyer pays the HOA a working-capital contribution (½ of a year's dues) + a reserve contribution (½ of a year's dues) = one full year of dues, both non-refundable and not credited against future dues. $594.34 × 12 = $7,132.08 — exactly the "capital improvement fee" on the SPDS. This is your cost at closing, not the seller's.
  • Delinquency & liens
    Dues unpaid within 15 days are delinquent and accrue 18% annual interest plus late fees, secured by an automatic assessment lien (perfected by the recorded declaration under A.R.S. § 33-1256). The board may also levy special assessments and reasonable fines for rule violations.
  • Insurance — what you must carry
    The HOA master policy covers the building and common elements but not your personal property or in-unit liability. You must carry an HO-6 (personal-property and liability) policy. The HOA can also pass its master-policy deductible through to you — for damage to your unit (any cause) or to other areas if caused by your negligence — so add loss-assessment and deductible coverage.
  • How long the rules last
    The covenants run with the land for a long initial term and auto-renew in ten-year periods — they bind you and every future owner. Amendments and certain board actions require defined owner and first-mortgagee approvals.
  • Jurisdiction
    The declaration is written to City of Scottsdale ordinances — permits, code, and short-term-rental rules run through Scottsdale, even though the 85253 ZIP carries a "Paradise Valley" postal name.
